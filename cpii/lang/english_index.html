Background

With the aging of the global population becoming increasingly serious, it is estimated that by 2050, the number of people aged 60 or above will double to 22% globally, and the number of people aged 65 or above in Hong Kong will increase to 35%.

Ageing has led to an increase in the number of highly burdensome geriatric syndromes and a rise in public healthcare expenditures, which, coupled with a declining workforce and a shrinking tax base, threaten the sustainability of our society.

Hong Kong as a highly urbanized region also faces unique challenges. The fast-paced urban lifestyle, highly competitive social environment and the spread of modern technology have brought new factors to bear on the lifestyle and health of the elderly.

To address this issue and against this backdrop, we believe that our innovative assessment platform will have a significant positive impact on the Hong Kong community.

Aim

Our aim is to develop a verbal biomarker, which is a non-invasive alternative to blood tests and brain scans for screening and monitoring of non-communicable diseases.

This approach allows for easy remote capture of spoken language data, enabling sensitive cognitive assessment through millisecond-resolution recording of speech events such as delays and disfluencies.

This study will support the World Health Organization's plan to make cognitive decline a national and international public health and social care priority to better support patients and caregivers in Hong Kong.

Custom Test

These are some tests that you can do on yourself to see if you have neurocognitive disorder.

If you have scored positive in any of these test then you might have neurocognitive disorder and may consider to use our technology to help you with that.

I. Forgetfulness Test

These questions are designed to assess whether older people often forget things, such as important dates, appointments, or important information.

II. Cognitive ability tests

Standardized cognitive ability tests, such as the Maze Test, Number Sequence Test, Verbal Comprehension Test, etc., are used to assess cognitive functioning and thinking ability.

III. Memory Test

Ask the elderly about themselves and assess whether they can remember past events and situations.

IV. Assessment of Daily Living Ability

To assess the elderly's ability to take care of themselves in daily life, such as cooking, bathing, dressing, and so on.

Plan

We plan to combine advanced artificial intelligence technology and verbal biomarkers to create an innovative assessment platform that plays a crucial role in NCD screening and monitoring.

We aim to develop an automated, objective, and user-friendly evaluation platform based on low-cost NCD biomarkers for screening and monitoring.

This platform will enable remote monitoring and personalized data collection for early detection of cognitive decline, thereby enhancing disease management efficiency, delaying institutionalization, and reducing caregiving costs.
